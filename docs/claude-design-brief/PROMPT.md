# First turn for Claude Design

Paste this as your opening message.

---

You are the design lead on Balam.AI, a family health AI coach. The founder wants the app to feel like a quiet-luxury service, not a clinical tool. Before you design a single screen, read the four files attached in this order: `BRAND-BRIEF.md`, `SCREENS.md`, `REFERENCES.md`, and then any PNG in `current-screens/`. They are short on purpose.

Direction, not decisions:

- Anchor aesthetic: Aman / Rosewood quiet luxury, filtered for a small mobile app. Editorial, warm, muted, restrained. Magazine-adjacent. Deep calm.
- Explicit anti-direction: no aggressive medical red for ordinary states. Red is reserved strictly for emergencies. No cartoon-baby UI. No dense medical dashboards. No grey Material shadows. No Shutterstock-style stock photography. No emoji in UI chrome.
- Typography direction: editorial serif for headlines in the family of Tiempos, GT Alpina, or Ogg; neutral-modern sans for body in the family of Inter, GT America, or Soehne. Pick the exact pairing and tell me what you picked and why.
- Color direction: the current app is coral-primary + soft-blue-secondary + honey-accent on a warm off-white. Feel free to re-propose the palette at a slightly lower saturation to sit better under editorial photography. Keep warmth. Document final tokens in a compact palette grid.
- Visual language: photography-first for hero moments and abstract shape or gradient for utility surfaces. Photography should feel like Apartamento or Kinfolk editorials, not stock parenting imagery. Faces should include Central Asian and diverse parents. Morning light. Hands-first. Candid.

Hard constraints:

- Canvas: 390 x 844 mobile.
- Modes: light first, dark as a side-by-side companion on every screen. Dark is not an afterthought.
- Trilingual: English, Russian, Kyrgyz. Russian and Kyrgyz run roughly 20-30 percent wider than English. Do not hard-code widths that break under text expansion.
- Bottom navigation stays 5 tabs: Today, Balam AI, Community, Market, My Child. Do not redesign navigation order.
- No emoji in chrome.

Output in this order:

1. Design system first. A compact palette grid (light + dark), a typography scale with the two faces you picked, radii scale, shadow system, icon style note. One page.
2. Today screen mockup (light + dark side by side, annotated with what changed vs the current screenshot I attached).
3. Short rationale for the Today screen: three sentences, no more.
4. Then stop. Wait for my feedback before continuing to screen 2. Faster loop beats batching eight.

When you stop, print: "Today v1 ready. Palette in message header. Open questions: ..." and list any.
