# Screens to design

Eight screens, light mode first, dark mode as a side-by-side companion. Design screens 1 and 2 first, stop, wait for feedback. Then unlock the rest.

## 1. Today, home

**Purpose.** The screen a user sees every morning for three minutes. Tells her what is worth her attention for the currently selected household member and points to one clear next action.

**Key content.** A compact greeting that adapts to the selected member. A horizontal row of household member avatars directly under the greeting with a clear selected state. A small grid of quick-access entries for Lab Results, Doctors, Moments or Medications depending on member role, and Log Vitals. Below that, three or four cards: one focus of the week, one thing to try today, one "is this normal?" entry into the AI, one daily insight. A settings gear and a notification bell in the top corner.

**The one thing it must do well.** In two seconds the user should know who the screen is about and what the next action is.

## 2. Balam AI chat, 3am dark variant

**Purpose.** The screen a parent opens at 2:47am when something is wrong. Must be calm even when the question is not.

**Key content.** A single conversational surface. A small chip above the input showing the member the conversation is about. Large type. Red-flag triage banner below a reply when urgency is high, with two actions: Consult a doctor and Call emergency services. No suggestion chips in 3am mode. Haptic on send. The demo money-shot.

**The one thing it must do well.** Never panic the user. Composed even when delivering an emergency recommendation.

## 3. Household, my family

**Purpose.** See everyone you track and add new members without friction.

**Key content.** A list of rich member cards: portrait or monogram, name, role, age, and one line of health pulse ("normal range this week", "BP trending high"). Tapping a card sets that member active across the whole app. An "add family member" button that opens a sheet with role chips: me, child, partner, mother, father, grandmother, grandfather, sibling, other. Stage and age controls adapt to the chosen role.

**The one thing it must do well.** Make it feel inviting to add a grandmother or an uncle, not bureaucratic.

## 4. Lab results, interpretive view

**Purpose.** Translate a lab report into something a non-doctor parent can act on, for the currently selected member.

**Key content.** A pills-row summary at top showing counts of normal, borderline, and flagged values. A list of parameters with soft traffic-light indicators (green for normal, amber for borderline, red only for true flags), name and value with unit. Tap to expand a one-sentence plain-language explanation. A prominent "view original lab report (PDF)" action near the top, not buried.

**The one thing it must do well.** Replace the spreadsheet feeling with a feeling of being briefed.

## 5. Consult a doctor

**Purpose.** Let the user pick a trusted specialist and start an async consult in one tap.

**Key content.** Doctor cards with portrait, name, specialty, languages spoken, response-time SLA, and price. One primary call-to-action per card: "Start consult". Specialty filter chips above the list. A 4-step intake flow with generous spacing and progress dots. Payment confirmation lives at step four, not before.

**The one thing it must do well.** Make the doctor feel like a named, respected person, not a lookup row.

## 6. Log vitals, adult member

**Purpose.** Let a daughter log her father's glucose in two seconds after his home test.

**Key content.** A focused modal sheet. Big numbers (for example, "120 over 80" for blood pressure). Trend arrows compared to the last reading. For glucose, a fasting / post-meal toggle. A full-width "Log" button. Medium-impact haptic on save. A one-line context under the number: "last glucose 118, two days ago".

**The one thing it must do well.** Feel faster than the Apple Health app. Two seconds end to end.

## 7. Onboarding, three screens

**Purpose.** Get a new user from App Store install to a meaningful first interaction in under 90 seconds.

**Key content.** Screen 1: a trilingual welcome, a single "get started" action, language visible and tappable in a corner. Screen 2: a stage picker presented as a quiz, not a form ("pregnant", "newborn", "toddler", "already parenting several", "just curious", "focused on my own health"). Screen 3: a short signup with name, email, password; iOS autofill hints wired.

**The one thing it must do well.** First screen has no form at all. Reading time to first tap is about three seconds.

## 8. Moments

**Purpose.** Capture firsts (first smile, first steps) and, later, share them with family members who are not in the app.

**Key content.** A calm editorial timeline of captured moments: a single large photo per item, a date, a one-line caption, an unobtrusive share action. An "add moment" action that opens a warm, generous capture sheet. Empty state feels like a pre-written page waiting for its first entry.

**The one thing it must do well.** Feel like flipping a well-printed photo album, not scrolling a social feed.
