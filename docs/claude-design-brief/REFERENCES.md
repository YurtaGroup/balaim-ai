# References

Study these. Then explicitly do not reference the other list.

## Study

**Apps and product surfaces.**

- Aman Resorts app: the way restrained typography, mutedness, and a single large image per screen carries emotion without adornment.
- Rosewood Hotels app: composure and pace; buttons that feel considered, not clicked through.
- Yours wellness app: calm, editorial parenting-adjacent design that does not fall into cartoon territory.
- Airbnb Luxe: how to make an expensive thing feel warm rather than sterile.
- Linear: type hierarchy, navigation minimalism, and how empty space does work.
- Notion: how to stay visually quiet even when a page is dense.

**Publications for photography direction and editorial voice.**

- Apartamento magazine: hands, natural light, lived-in rooms, unperformed moments.
- Kinfolk magazine: whitespace as a material, morning light, restraint.
- The New York Times T Magazine: captions and small labels that feel edited, not auto-generated.
- Apple Newsroom editorial: large leading images, generous margins.
- Mast Brothers packaging: how a warm off-white and a serif become a brand.

## Do not reference

- Babylist. BabyCenter. The Bump. Glow. Ovia. (Busy parenting-app homes, too many tiles, cartoony palettes, aggressive color.)
- WebMD and any clinical symptom-checker UI. (Dense, alarming, red-first.)
- Headspace's illustrated bubble style. (Friendly but wrong register for medical.)
- Apple Health's default look. (Too cold, too chart-forward.)
- Any Material Design tutorial dashboard. (Grey shadows, tonal chips, default M3 everything.)
- Stock photography libraries that feature smiling mothers holding babies in front of white backgrounds. (Faceless, sanitized, not our family.)
- Medical-monitoring dashboards used in hospitals. (ICU vibe.)
- Figma default starter kits for parenting apps. (Everyone uses them; we should not look like anyone.)

## Photography specifics

Warm morning light. Central Asian and diverse families. Hands first (a hand holding a bottle, a hand on a forehead, a hand on a cup of tea). Candid, not posed. A slight grain is welcome. No studio lighting. No performative smiles. No baby models in white onesies against a white backdrop.

## Typography specifics

Editorial serif for display and headlines in the family of Tiempos, GT Alpina, or Ogg. Neutral modern sans for body in the family of Inter, GT America, or Soehne. The final pairing is the designer's call; announce the choice with a one-sentence rationale.

## Tone specifics

Calm, restrained, edited. Captions feel written, not generated. Error states are short and respectful. "Call emergency services" is a statement, not a warning.
