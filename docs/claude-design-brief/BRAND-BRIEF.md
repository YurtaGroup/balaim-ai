# Brand brief

## Positioning, one line

Balam is your family's quiet, kind AI health coach. It starts with the baby and grows with the whole household.

## Product, in plain terms

An iPhone app that lets a parent manage their family's health in one place. The baby has tracking and a parenting AI. The parent gets the same AI as a personal health coach. A grandparent or partner can be added as a household member. Every member has their own labs, their own vitals, their own consults with real physicians. The AI knows which family member you are asking about and speaks with the right tone: gentle with the baby, precise with the adult.

## User

A parent aged 25 to 45, based in Kyrgyzstan or the diaspora, raising one to three children. She code-switches between English, Russian, and Kyrgyz in the same sentence. She owns an iPhone. She is often the person managing not only her children's health but her own and her parents' too, without a good system to do it in. She is time-starved, sometimes scared at 3am, rarely impressed by dashboards. She wants reassurance and competence in the same breath.

## Emotional tone

Quiet, warm, deliberate. Like sitting on a hotel terrace with morning coffee before anyone else is awake. The opposite of a clinical waiting room. The opposite of a gamified wellness app. The opposite of another parenting-app bubble-font home screen.

## What this must never feel like

- A medical dashboard that greets you with numbers.
- A cartoon baby world with rainbow gradients and rounded sticker icons.
- An Apple Health clone full of charts.
- A Shutterstock mood board of generic smiling mothers.
- A developer's idea of Material Design.
- A language app with flags and chips where localization feels bolted on.

## Why now

The founder's uncle died recently of unchecked diabetes. That is the personal reason the product is expanding from parenting into family health. The redesign must carry that weight without ever stating it. The feeling should be: this app is the thing that makes you notice before it is too late. Calm, not alarming. Present, not pushy.

## Name

Balam. In Kyrgyz it means my child. The brand starts there and widens. Do not propose renaming.
