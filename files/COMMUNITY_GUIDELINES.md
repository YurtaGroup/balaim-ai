# Community Guidelines — "Ask the Village"

## Our Community Promise
balam.ai's community is the safest, warmest, most honest space for mothers in Kyrgyzstan.
Every mom who posts deserves a helpful, non-judgmental, respectful response.

---

## The Golden Rules

### For Everyone
1. **Be kind** — You don't know what this mom's night was like
2. **Share experience, not diagnosis** — "My baby had something similar..." not "Your baby has X"
3. **No judgment on parenting choices** — breast vs formula, co-sleep vs crib, all welcome
4. **Photos of babies are sacred** — never screenshot, never share outside the app
5. **Tag content correctly** — helps the right moms and experts find it

### For Posting
- Describe the issue clearly (the more detail, the better answers)
- Photos encouraged for health questions — they dramatically improve response quality
- One question per post (keeps responses focused)
- Update your post when resolved — helps future moms searching for the same issue

### For Responding
- "I've been through this" stories are gold — be specific and honest
- If you don't know, say so — don't guess on health questions
- Upvote answers that genuinely helped (signals quality to others)
- Never post links to external products in responses (that's what the marketplace is for)

---

## Photo Guidelines

### Allowed
- Baby photos showing health concerns (rash, swelling, eye discharge, etc.)
- Context photos (nursery setup, feeding position, etc.)
- Celebration photos (birthday cakes, milestone moments)

### Not Allowed (Auto-removed + reported)
- Photos of baby's private areas — ever, for any reason
- Photos of other people's children without consent
- Graphic wound photos that could be distressing without content warning
- Screenshots from other apps or private conversations

---

## Content Standards

### Immediately Removed
- Medical misinformation (AI + human moderated)
- Promotion of harmful "remedies" (e.g., giving honey to infants under 1 year)
- MLM product promotion, spam, affiliate links
- Bullying, shaming, or personal attacks
- Content that sexualizes children in any way

### Flagged for Human Review
- Any post suggesting a potentially serious medical emergency
- Reports of possible child abuse or neglect
- Posts indicating postpartum depression crisis
- Disputes about medical advice

---

## Emergency Protocol
If a post mentions any of:
- Difficulty breathing
- Blue lips or fingertips
- Unresponsive baby
- Seizure
- High fever (>38.5°C in baby under 3 months)
- Severe allergic reaction

→ AI immediately shows: **"⚠️ Call 103 (Emergency) now. Do not wait for community response."**
→ Post is flagged for moderator + expert notification

---

## Postpartum Mental Health Protocol
If a post mentions:
- "I don't want to be a mother"
- "I can't do this anymore"
- Thoughts of harming self or baby
- "I wish I hadn't had a baby"

→ AI responds with: warm, non-alarmist message + psychologist contact + "You are not alone, this is more common than you think"
→ Post is flagged for psychologist review (priority)
→ Anonymous mode enabled automatically for these posts

---

## Moderation Team

### MVP
- 1 part-time community moderator (3–4 hours/day)
- AI pre-screening (auto-remove clear violations, flag ambiguous)
- Pediatrician reviews flagged health posts

### Scale (Phase 2)
- 2 full-time moderators
- Expert team has direct moderation access
- Community trust system: long-standing helpful members get "Trusted Mom" badge + lighter moderation

---

## Reporting
Every post and reply has a "Flag" button.
Reports go to:
1. AI triage (immediate)
2. Moderator queue (within 2 hours)
3. Expert notification (for medical flags)

Reporter stays anonymous. Reported user is not notified of who reported them.
